Gauging a Lifetime on Skid Rho

Sun Seekers — 61 Orders of Magnitude

A clock of how long things get away with existing. Sixty-one powers of ten. From Planck flicker to stellar patience.

Why Planck Time?

Three fundamental constants — ħ (quantum of action), G (gravity), c (light speed) — yield the unique combination with units of time:

t_P = √(ħG/c⁵) = √([kg·m²s⁻¹][m³kg⁻¹s⁻²]/[ms⁻¹]⁵) = √(s²) ✓ ≈ 5.39×10⁻⁴⁴ s

The universe is ~10⁶¹ × 5.4×10⁻⁴⁴ s old. A clock with one tick per order gives exactly 61 positions, far more natural than an arbitrary speedometer axis.

Little old (transient) mesons, all on their ownsome, flare for 10⁻²⁴ seconds and are gone: each decay a small contribution to the universe's long slide toward equilibrium. The mesons alone span sixteen orders of magnitude: from the ρ meson at 4.4×10⁻²⁴ s (~4 o'clock) to the π pion at 2.6×10⁻⁸ s (~7 o'clock). Reduce the possible channels of your demise and you live longer.

A free neutron lasts fifteen minutes in isolation; bind it into a nucleus and it outlasts civilisations. Aggregated cellular matter in human form is nearly 33 orders of magnitude less ephemeral than a rho meson. Lumpiness, it turns out, is a survival strategy.

Biology is different. Life announces itself to the universe by its entropy signature: the capture of high-energy photons and their conversion into many more lower-energy ones. We do not resist the second law, rather we accelerate it, locally as we organise ourselves.

Trophic ladders leak energy at every rung. Efficiency is survival. Closer to the photon stream, fewer losses. If you eat photons either second or third hand in bulk you can live longer than your mass alone suggests.

The Sun runs the pures model: mass to light, almost no intermediaries. Not quite ketosis in any biochemical sense, rather a fusion metabolism, clean and direct. Its main-sequence lifetime at ~1017.5 s sits at 11.9 o'clock, nearly closing the full revolution back to Planck time. We humans are sun seeking chemical bags, photon eaters several steps removed.

The dial asks only this: does endurance belong to those who simplify the ways they end or to those closest to the fire?
